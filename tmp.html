Well Made Co. is a camping company that prides itself in well-made products and eco-friendly everything by creating products that last forever. The client wanted the packaging that those products come in to also live longer than long enough to dump into a trash can. The project called for us to create useful second lives for the packaging beyond informing customers about the product.

The problem was dual-fold: they needed a logo design that fit their quaint, rustic personality, and they needed packaging that could be useful on the campsite or while hiking. The project involved a lot of visual explortion to hone in on how to represent that visually so that customers would understand what Well Made Co. was all about.

Well Made Co. came with lots of visuals that they liked and looked to us for how they could translate that into a brand. After a lot of sketching and photographic research, the decision was to create a logo system of woodland animals that they could use to separate the different categories of products they sold.

The final logo had various different lockups to account for the different products the logo would live on. The logotype designs captured some of the rustic, manifest destiny feel that suits the outdoors while the bear provided a metaphor for ursa major and the North Star. The bear and its reference to the north star became a way for Well Made Co. to say: when you're with Well Made Co., you're never lost.

The logo was mocked up on all sorts of packaging to hone in on the three that made the final cut: product tags, store bags, and tubes for storing trinkets.

The product tags served the dual purpose of not only being traditional product tags in the store but also keychains with little sky maps that would glow in the dark. It went back to the idea that with Well Made Co., you were never lost: they even gave you the tools to find north in the dark.

The store tubes, which could be used for carrying vertical items out of the store, doubled as a way to keep maps dry while on hikes.

The store bags had a second life as bear hangs: each bag had instructions on how to use it to keep food away from the campsite so that pesky critters wouldn't bother you or your food.
